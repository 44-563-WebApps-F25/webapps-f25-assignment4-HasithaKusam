Hasitha Kusam - Light Opera

The Mikado

Act I, Scene I

(A public square in the town of Titipu. Gentlemen of Japan discovered.)

NANKI-POO: A wandering minstrel I— a thing of shreds and patches.

CHORUS: Of ballads, songs, and snatches, and dreamy lullaby!

NANKI-POO: My catalogue is long, through every passion ranging.

CHORUS: And to your humbler, weaker thoughts, I’ll lend an easy cry.

NANKI-POO: I tune my supple song to suit the vocal strength of others.

CHORUS: When anguish wrings the heart, or when the soul is full of laughter!

NANKI-POO: For he who’d charm both old and young must make his music matter!

(They bow ceremoniously.)
